About Us

Founded in 2024, Michigan Quantitative Computing is an innovative project-based mathematical computing club at the University of Michigan

Our Purpose

MQC was formed to be an outlet for students with a passion for mathematics and computer science to practice their skills in real-world contexts.

Members have the opportunity to form and join a different project team every semester. Covering topics from statistical arbitrage to protein synthesis to neural networks and public database word processing, these teams can explore infinite possible methods to processing and understanding scientific and economic data. As long as your team can write the algorithm, there is no idea too crazy to test!

In order to encourage members to take risks and explore uncharted topics, we backtest all financial algorithms over years of data, as opposed to holding an active fund. This allows us to uphold a comfortable, stress-free environment within our community.

Why Join Us

Technical Experience

Our primary focus as a club is to equip our members with a greater understanding of quantitative methods in STEM fields ranging from finance to the natural sciences. Because of our semester-long project team framework, you will have the opportunity to explore a multitude of different topics throughout your membership. No matter what topic under the umbrella of STEM you want to gain experience with, you can do so through MQC.

Welcoming Community

What really makes MQC great is the emphasis on community. You'll find that every member who is extremely passionate about their area of interest is equally passionate about introducing new people to it. Through club events and working with your project teams, you'll have the opportunity to get to know and spend time with people from all different backgrounds who share the same passion for CS / Math as you.

Low Pressure Learning

One of the foundational goals of MQC is to provide a space where students can comfortably make mistakes and explore new topics without a fear of failure. By taking steps like eliminating professional dress from meetings and backtesting finance algorithms without real money, we've set out to make sure that there are no external pressures impeding any member's ability to learn.
